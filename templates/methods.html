Methodology & Data Sources

The Haystack is built on the philosophy of high-trust, localized intelligence. We pull from federal, state, and local datasets to build an accurate picture of the Kansas City regional training and labor market. Because these datasets are rarely clean or perfectly interoperable, this page details exactly where our data comes from and the assumptions we make.

Educational Pipeline (Phase 1)

IPEDS (National Center for Education Statistics)

We ingest 12 individual data files from the Fall 2024 IPEDS collection. This represents Title IV accredited institutions. We pull graduation rates (150% and 200%), net price, cost of attendance, enrollment demographics, and program-level completions.

* Note: Program-level data uses CIP codes which we crosswalk to SOC occupational codes.

WIOA ETPL

Eligible Training Provider Lists for both Kansas and Missouri. This brings non-Title IV short-term and technical training onto the platform. Since WIOA uses proprietary program structures, we use a fuzzy-matching entity reconciliation algorithm to merge WIOA providers with existing IPEDS institutions when possible.

Registered Apprenticeships

Sourced from the Department of Labor Partner Finder. We geofence apprenticeship sponsors to the KC MSA via ZIP codes. Because DOL data often lacks structured occupation codes, some apprenticeship-to-career links are manually curated or inferred.

Workforce Intelligence (Phase 2)

Occupational Earnings (BLS OEWS)

We use the Bureau of Labor Statistics Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics. We prioritize local Kansas City MSA data for all wage bands. If a specific occupation is extremely rare locally and the BLS suppresses its data to protect employer privacy, we explicitly fallback to National data and tag it with (Nat.).

Employment Projections (BLS EP)

We integrate the BLS 2024–2034 10-year employment projections. Because the BLS does not publish 10-year forecasts for localized metro areas, our long-term trajectory percentage is based on the national forecast. This tracks both total employment growth and annual openings (replacement volume).

Kansas City Industry Momentum (BLS QCEW)

To provide a fast-moving signal of local demand, we ingest the Quarterly Census of Employment and Wages for the KC Metro counties. We run a trailing 3-year linear regression on Private Sector (Ownership Code 5) industry employment. If an industry is growing locally, the occupations that staff that industry receive a positive momentum signal via our NAICS-to-SOC Matrix integration.

Regional Talent Flows (Census LEHD J2J)

To surface actionable career pivot strategies, we integrate the US Census Bureau's Longitudinal Employer-Household Dynamics (LEHD) Job-to-Job Flows dataset. This leverages state administrative UI wage records to track where workers actually go when they change industries. We filter this matrix geographically to Missouri and Kansas to pinpoint regional "Talent Magnets" and construct data-backed "Alternative Career Pathways" for workers looking to transition out of their current sector.

O*NET Ontology

Sourced from the US Department of Labor. We use O*NET Job Zones (Education/Experience levels 1-5), Core Skills, and Alternate Titles (to power plain-language search). O*NET's "Bright Outlook" is natively integrated to highlight careers with massive national upside.

Automation Risk & Remote Potential

To assess future-proofing, we import two specialized academic datasets: the Frey & Osborne Automation Probability matrix (evaluating routine cognitive/manual tasks susceptible to computerization) and the Dingel & Neiman Teleworkability dataset (assessing whether tasks can be feasibly performed from home).

College Scorecard

Sourced from the Department of Education. We link our programs to College Scorecard data to surface outcomes such as median debt at graduation and earnings post-graduation. This connects the cost of training to the expected ROI.

KC Career Intelligence Algorithms

Outlook "Career Grade" Matrix

Beta

The Outlook dashboard assigns a proprietary Career Grade to every SOC occupation. This grade acts as a weighted composite score designed to evaluate a career's overall vitality in the KC region. Every occupation is ranked on a 0-100 percentile scale against all other careers before weights are applied. Missing data points default to average percentiles to prevent undue penalization.

Factor Weight Source
Demand Volume 25% BLS Projections (Annual Openings)
Compensation 20% BLS OEWS (KC Median Wage)
Demand Trajectory 20% BLS Projections (10-yr Growth)
Local KC Signal 15% BLS QCEW (Trailing Momentum of Top Industry)
Fast ROI 10% Wage divided by O*NET Job Zone
