How to read this data

What are "completions"?

Completions are awards conferred during the academic year — degrees, certificates, and other credentials actually earned by students. This is not the same as enrollment or graduation rates. A single student can earn multiple awards in one year.

Why do some programs show — instead of a number?

NCES suppresses completion values when fewer than 3 students completed a program in a given year, to protect student privacy. A dash (—) means data exists but is withheld. It does not mean zero completions.

How are programs linked to occupations?

The occupation links are derived from the NCES CIP 2020 → SOC 2018 crosswalk. Each program's 6-digit CIP code (Classification of Instructional Programs) is matched to one or more SOC codes (Standard Occupational Classification) from the crosswalk. This is a structural mapping, not a guaranteed employment outcome.

What is the KC MSA?

The Kansas City Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) includes {{ org.county_fips and 'the county where this institution is located, plus' or '' }} counties in both Missouri and Kansas defined by the U.S. Office of Management and Budget. The Haystack filters to institutions whose primary campus is within these counties.

Data currency

Institutional data is sourced from IPEDS 2023–24 (data year 2024). IPEDS data is collected annually; this reflects the most recent finalized release available.
