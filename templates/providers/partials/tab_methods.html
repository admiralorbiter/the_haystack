How to read this data

What are "completions"?

Completions are awards conferred during the academic year — degrees, certificates, and other credentials actually earned by students. This is not the same as enrollment or graduation rates. A single student can earn multiple awards in one year.

What is the "retention" rate?

Retention measures the percentage of first-time, degree/certificate-seeking undergraduate students from the previous fall who either re-enrolled or successfully completed their program by the current fall. Depending on the institution's predominant student body, this is displayed for either full-time (FT) or part-time (PT) cohorts.

How is "Local Sticker Price" calculated?

Because NCES IPEDS data does not report average grant/scholarship aid broken down by living arrangement, we cannot accurately report a single "net price" that applies exclusively to commuter students. Instead, we provide a Local Sticker Price, which represents the total direct, advertised cost for a student living at home. It is calculated as the sum of In-State Tuition & Fees and Books & Supplies, before any financial aid is applied. It deliberately excludes estimated living expenses like transportation and food to reflect only the direct educational costs.

Why do some programs show — instead of a number?

NCES suppresses completion values when fewer than 3 students completed a program in a given year, to protect student privacy. A dash (—) means data exists but is withheld. It does not mean zero completions.

How are programs linked to occupations?

The occupation links are derived from the NCES CIP 2020 → SOC 2018 crosswalk. Each program's 6-digit CIP code (Classification of Instructional Programs) is matched to one or more SOC codes (Standard Occupational Classification) from the crosswalk. This is a structural mapping, not a guaranteed employment outcome.

What is the KC MSA?

The Haystack filters to institutions whose primary campus is within these counties.

Data currency

Institutional data is sourced from IPEDS 2023–24 (data year 2024). IPEDS data is collected annually; this reflects the most recent finalized release available.
