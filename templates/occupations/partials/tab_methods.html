Data Methods

  • Wages & Employment: This data originates from the Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OEWS) dataset compiled by the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS). It surveys establishments across the country. Local wage data applies specifically to the Kansas City MSA. Data is valid as of May 2024.
  • Preparation & Job Zones: Job Zone categories (1-5) classify how much education, experience, and on-the-job training an occupation requires. This is sourced directly from the O*NET version 29.0 database sponsored by the U.S. Department of Labor.
  • Training Paths: The connections between Kansas City training programs and occupations rely on the federally standardized 'CIP-to-SOC Crosswalk' (2020 edition) managed by the National Center for Education Statistics (NCES).
  • Talent Pipelines & Who Hires This: The primary matching from an occupation to specific industries leverages the National BLS Industry-Occupation wage matrix. The directional tracking for "Alternative Career Pathways" when leaving a sector utilizes the Census Bureau's LEHD Job-to-Job (J2J) Flows dataset, filtered geographically to our state boundaries over the trailing four quarters.
ℹ️
Some wage bands (e.g., 75th percentile) or specific employment numbers may be suppressed (—) to protect the confidentiality of surveyed employers, particularly for small or highly-specialized occupations.

High-Earning Occupations (*): The BLS OEWS methodology caps reported median wages at around $239,200 annually. If the median exceeds this wage ceiling, it is suppressed. When a median is suppressed but an annual mean is available, Haystack displays the Mean wage instead and denotes it with an asterisk.
