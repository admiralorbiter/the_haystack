How to read this data

What is a CIP family?

CIP stands for Classification of Instructional Programs — a taxonomy maintained by NCES to standardize how educational programs are categorized. The 2-digit family code ({{ cip_family }} — {{ family_label }}) groups related programs into a broad field of study. Full 6-digit CIP codes identify specific programs within that field.

Why are programs linked to certain occupations?

The occupation links on this page come from the NCES CIP 2020 → SOC 2018 crosswalk. Each program's 6-digit CIP code is matched to one or more Standard Occupational Classification (SOC) codes based on a structural mapping maintained by NCES. This reflects typical career pathways — it is not a guaranteed employment outcome.

What do the "Programs" counts mean on the Occupations tab?

The Programs count shows how many individual training programs in the {{ family_label }} field map to a given occupation. A higher count means the occupation is broadly connected across many programs in this field — a proxy for structural relevance, not labor demand.

What are "completions"?

Completions are awards conferred during the academic year — degrees, certificates, and other credentials actually earned by students. This is not the same as enrollment or graduation rates. A single student can earn multiple awards in one year.

Why do some programs show — instead of a number?

NCES suppresses completion values when fewer than 3 students completed a program in a given year, to protect student privacy. A dash (—) means data exists but is withheld by NCES. It does not mean zero completions.

Data currency

Program and completion data is sourced from IPEDS Completions (C) 2023–24 (data year 2024). CIP→SOC crosswalk is NCES CIP 2020 / SOC 2018. IPEDS data is collected annually; this reflects the most recent finalized release available.
